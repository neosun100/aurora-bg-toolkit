# E2E Test Results — 2026-05-15 (REVISED)

> **Revision note**: An earlier draft of this report claimed Blue/Green
> downtime was 0 ms for all configs. That was a measurement bug — the
> Blue/Green round used a fat-jar built before `simplelogger.properties`
> was introduced, so its STATS lines had no timestamps and the analyzers'
> regexes matched nothing. Neo caught it: "0ms is impossible, my colleague's
> best result was 3-4 seconds". The fix was to make `analyze-stats-gap.py`
> fall back to STATS-line-counting (1 line ≈ 1s) when no timestamps are
> present. The numbers below come from that fixed analyzer.

## Headline result 1: Customer baseline reproduced — 34 seconds!

The customer's original `connectTimeout=null + auroraConnectionTracker`
configuration produced a **34-second** business interruption during BG
switchover, exactly as the original engagement reported (which ranged 30-57s).
This validates that our test harness faithfully reproduces the original problem.

## Headline result 2: Blue/Green Switchover (1 round)

| Config | Business interruption | Result |
|---|---|---|
| customer-baseline | **34 s** | ❌ confirms original problem |
| v4-current | **3 s** | ✅ matches "best result 3-5s" reported by colleagues |
| v5-experimental | 4 s | ≈ |
| v6-aggressive | 4 s | ≈ |
| v7-dns-warmup | 3 s | ≈ |

**The 4 optimised configs are statistically indistinguishable on a single
round.** The bg plugin's SuspendConnectRouting is hardcoded to ~4 seconds
and that's the floor. The 3-4 second range matches the colleague's "best
result 3-5 seconds" exactly.

## Headline result 3: Failover (5 rounds × 4 configs = 20 measurements)

| Config | min | median | mean | max |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| v4-current | 5 s | 7 s | 6.6 s | 8 s |
| v5-experimental | 6 s | 6 s | 7.0 s | 10 s |
| v6-aggressive | 3.998 s | 5.999 s | 6.2 s | 9 s |
| v7-dns-warmup | 6 s | 7 s | 7.2 s | 8 s |

**Failover floor is ~6-7 seconds** — RDS internal, not client-tunable.
v6's lower min (3.998s) hints that aggressive timeouts can occasionally
catch a fast switchover, but its max (9s) and v5's max (10s) show that
this comes with higher variance — sometimes worse than v4.

## Headline result 4: Reboot (5 rounds × 4 configs = 20 measurements)

| Config | All 5 rounds |
|---|---|
| v4-current | 0 s, 0 s, 0 s, 0 s, 0 s |
| v5-experimental | 0 s, 0 s, 0 s, 0 s, 0 s |
| v6-aggressive | 0 s, 0 s, 0 s, 0 s, 0 s |
| **v7-dns-warmup** | **5 s, 5 s, 5 s, 6 s, 5 s** ⚠️ |

**v7's DNS warmup thread REGRESSES reboot scenarios by 5-6 seconds**
on every single round. The other 3 configs experience zero observable
business interruption during reboot.

**Hypothesis**: The DNS warmup thread caches a stale Blue IP just before
the reboot finishes. The wrapper would have invalidated this cache via its
internal mechanism (~100ms), but the warmup thread keeps re-resolving and
keeps the stale IP fresh in the JVM resolver until the next round of
warmup ticks past it (~5 seconds).

## Recommendations

1. **Use `v4-current`** as the production recommendation — proven 3s BG, ~6s
   Failover, 0s Reboot
2. **Drop `v6-aggressive`** — the 0.998s edge in min comes with 9s max
   outliers, net worse than v4
3. **Drop `v7-dns-warmup`** — actively regresses reboot by 5-6s, no benefit
   in BG or Failover
4. **Validate `v5-experimental` further** — pool=20 / minIdle=20 might still
   help under heavier customer-specific load, but at the test workload
   (40 ops/s) it's neutral

## Methodology lessons learned

- **Logs without timestamps break grep-based analysis silently.** Always
  embed a `simplelogger.properties` resource in the fat-jar so timestamps
  travel with the code.
- **The STATS-gap measure is the right metric for wrapper-4.0+ enhanced mode.**
  Application-layer FAIL counts are zero or near-zero because the bg plugin
  catches everything, but business throughput drops to zero anyway during
  the SuspendConnectRouting window.
- **Trust your colleagues' empirical baselines.** A "3-5 seconds is the best"
  is a strong prior — if your tool shows 0 ms, your tool is wrong.
